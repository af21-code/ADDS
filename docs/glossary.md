# Glossary

This glossary defines shared terminology for ADDS. Terms should be used
consistently across documentation, configuration, logs, plots, and future code.

## ADDS

Adaptive Drivetrain Decoupling System. A supervisory control concept that decides
when a vehicle drivetrain should remain connected, decouple for coasting, and
re-engage through rev-matching.

## Conventional Vehicle

The baseline vehicle configuration. It uses the same physical vehicle parameters
as the ADDS vehicle but does not have adaptive drivetrain decoupling authority.
When a drive gear is engaged and the coupling is closed, engine speed is linked
to wheel speed by the selected ratio.

## ADDS Vehicle

The comparison vehicle configuration. It uses the same physical vehicle
parameters as the conventional vehicle, but its controller may command
drivetrain decoupling, rev-matching, and controlled re-engagement.

## Drivetrain Coupling

The controllable mechanical interface between the engine side and the wheel side
of the drivetrain. Depending on the modeled vehicle, this may represent a clutch,
disconnect clutch, coupling, or equivalent device.

## Connected

An operating mode in which engine torque and engine braking can pass through the
drivetrain to the driven wheels.

## Decoupling

The controlled transition from connected operation toward a state in which the
engine and wheel-side drivetrain are mechanically separated.

## Decoupled

An operating mode in which the vehicle coasts while engine speed and wheel speed
evolve independently.

## Rev-Matching

The process of controlling engine speed toward the synchronous speed required for
smooth re-engagement in the target gear.

## Re-Engaging

The controlled transition from decoupled operation back to connected operation.
The transition should limit speed mismatch, torque disturbance, slip energy, and
longitudinal jerk.

## Synchronous Engine Speed

The engine speed that would match wheel speed for a selected gear and final-drive
ratio with zero coupling slip.

## Slip Speed

The angular speed difference across the coupling:

```text
slip_speed = engine_speed - synchronous_engine_speed
```

## Slip Energy

The energy dissipated by the coupling during speed mismatch. It is used as an
initial proxy for thermal load and wear.

## Engine Braking

Negative wheel-side torque caused by the connected engine and drivetrain during
accelerator lift or overrun.

## Overrun Fuel Cut-Off

An engine operating condition in which fuel injection is reduced to zero or near
zero while the engine is driven by vehicle motion through the drivetrain.

## Idle Fuel Use

Fuel consumed to keep the engine running while it is not producing useful
tractive power. Initial ADDS studies should include idle fuel use while
decoupled.

## Driver Demand

The requested acceleration, braking, or speed-following behavior generated by a
drive-cycle tracker, driver model, or recorded input trace.

## Route Preview

Information about upcoming target speed, road grade, curvature, or events. Route
preview must be explicitly declared so that controller comparisons do not use
future information unfairly.

## Supervisory Controller

The high-level controller that requests drivetrain mode changes, target gear, and
transition references.

## Safety Supervisor

A deterministic layer that validates controller requests, enforces transition
guards, applies action masks, and selects fallback behavior when needed.

## Fault-Safe Mode

A conservative fallback mode entered after invalid state, unsafe command,
transition failure, numerical failure, or unsupported operating condition.

## Scenario

A complete definition of route, target speed, driver demand, environmental
conditions, initial state, disturbances, and termination conditions.

## Metric

A computed quantity used to compare runs, such as fuel consumed, speed error,
jerk, response delay, or coupling slip energy.

## Baseline

A reference controller or vehicle behavior used for comparison. Baselines include
the conventional controller, a rule-based ADDS controller, and optional
optimization-based references.
